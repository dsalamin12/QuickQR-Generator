📚 QR Code Guide & Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about creating, customising, and using QR codes effectively.

What is the difference between PNG and SVG?▼
PNG is pixel-based — great for websites, emails, and screens. SVG is vector-based, scaling perfectly to any size without ever getting blurry — ideal for printing on banners, business cards, or merchandise.
Why use High error correction when adding a logo?▼
A logo placed in the center covers part of the QR pattern. High error correction lets scanners recover up to 30% of obscured data, keeping your code reliably scannable. QuickQR automatically enables this when you upload a logo.
Are QuickQR codes permanent?▼
Yes. The data is embedded directly in the QR pattern — no third-party service, no expiry, no tracking. The code works forever as long as the linked content (e.g. a URL) stays accessible.
How to make a Wi-Fi QR code for my café or office?▼
Select the Wi-Fi tab, type your network name and password, choose your security type (usually WPA/WPA2), customise the design, then download. Customers scan and connect instantly — no password typing needed.
Can I track how many times my QR code is scanned?▼
QuickQR creates static QR codes without built-in tracking. For scan analytics, create a tracked short link first using Bitly, Rebrandly, or Google UTM parameters, then paste that link into QuickQR. The redirect service counts the scans.
