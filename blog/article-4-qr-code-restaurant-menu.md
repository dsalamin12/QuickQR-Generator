## How a QR Code Menu Works

It's simpler than it sounds:

1. You put your menu online (as a PDF, Google Drive link, or a webpage)
2. You generate a QR code pointing to that link
3. You print the QR code and place it on your tables
4. Customers scan it with their phone camera → menu opens instantly

That's it. No app needed for the customer. No monthly subscription for you (when you use QuickQR).

## Step 1: Put Your Menu Online

Before making the QR code, you need a link to your menu. Here are 3 free ways to do that:

### Option A: Upload as a PDF to Google Drive (Easiest)
1. Open [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com) — free with any Google account
2. Upload your menu as a PDF file
3. Right-click the file → **Share** → set to *"Anyone with the link can view"*
4. Copy the link

That's your menu URL. Done.

### Option B: Use Google Docs
1. Type your menu in a Google Doc
2. Click **Share** → *"Anyone with the link can view"*
3. Copy the link

### Option C: Use a Free Website Builder
If you want a proper mobile menu page, [Canva](https://canva.com) has free menu templates you can publish as a webpage. This looks the most professional on a customer's phone.

> **Tip:** Whichever option you choose, open the link on your own phone first and check it looks good on a small screen. Customers will be viewing it on mobile.

## Step 5: Print and Place

### What to print it on:
- **Table tent card** — A folded card that stands on the table. Most common format.
- **Sticker** — Stick directly on the table surface or menu holder
- **Coaster** — Printed coasters with your QR code look smart and double as decoration
- **Laminated card** — More durable for regular use; easy to wipe clean

### What size should the QR code be?
For a table card, **5cm × 5cm** (about 2 inches square) is the ideal minimum. Larger is always fine. Smaller and some phones may struggle to scan from a comfortable distance.

### Add a short instruction:
First-time customers may not immediately know to scan it. Add a small label like:

> 📱 *Scan to view our menu*

Just that one line removes all hesitation.

## How to Update Your Menu Later

This is the best part of a QR code menu: **you never need to reprint the QR code**.

Since the QR code points to your Google Drive link or webpage, you just update the file at that link — the QR code automatically shows the new menu. Change prices, add seasonal dishes, remove sold-out items — all without reprinting a single card.

The only time you need a new QR code is if you change the link itself (e.g. switch from Google Drive to a new website).

## Tips for the Best Results

**Test on multiple phones.** Scan with both an iPhone and an Android before printing in bulk.

**Keep the menu link short.** Long Google Drive links still work, but if you ever get a custom domain, a short branded URL scans more reliably and looks better.

**Use high contrast.** Black dots on white background is the most reliable. Avoid light dots on coloured backgrounds unless you test thoroughly.

**Check loading speed.** Open the menu link on mobile data (not WiFi) to check it loads fast. A slow-loading menu frustrates customers.

**Update regularly.** A QR code menu that's always current builds customer trust. An outdated menu erodes it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the customer need to download an app?**
No. On all modern iPhones and Android phones, the built-in camera app scans QR codes directly. No separate app needed.

**What if I don't have a menu as a PDF?**
You can type your menu into a Google Doc and share that link. It doesn't need to be a PDF — any shareable web link works.

**Can I use this for a drinks menu or specials board too?**
Absolutely. Many restaurants use one QR code for the main menu and a second QR code for daily specials or the drinks menu. Each just points to a different link.

**What if my internet goes down?**
The QR code requires the customer's phone to have internet access to load the menu. For this reason, keeping a small stack of physical menus as backup is sensible for offline situations.
