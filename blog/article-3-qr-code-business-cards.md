## What Is a vCard QR Code?

A vCard QR code is a special QR code that stores contact information in a standard format (called vCard or VCF). When someone scans it:

1. Their phone reads all your details from the QR code
2. A prompt appears: *"Add to Contacts?"*
3. They tap Yes — and your complete profile is saved

It supports: full name, phone number, email address, company name, job title, and website.

## Design Tips for Business Card QR Codes

**Size matters.** On a standard business card, your QR code should be at least **2cm × 2cm** (about 0.8 inches square). Smaller than this and some phones struggle to scan it.

**Leave a white border.** QR codes need a small quiet zone (white border) around them to scan correctly. When placing in your design, make sure there's a few millimetres of white space around the code.

**Put it on the back.** The front of your card is for your name, title, and company. The back is the perfect home for the QR code — with a small label like "Scan to save my contact" or just a small phone icon above it.

**Test before ordering 500 cards.** Always print a single test copy (or test on screen) and scan it with your phone before placing a bulk order.

**Contrast is everything.** Never print a light-coloured QR code on a dark background unless you've tested it carefully. Some printers can't reproduce fine dark dots on coloured stock. When in doubt, black dots on white is always reliable.

## QR Code vs NFC Card — Which Is Better?

You may have heard about NFC (Near Field Communication) business cards — digital cards you tap to phones. Here's a quick comparison:

| Feature | QR Code Business Card | NFC Card |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost** | Free (just print cost) | $10–$30 per card |
| **Works on** | All smartphones (camera) | Most modern phones |
| **Sharable on photos/flyers** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Works on paper print** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Internet needed** | ❌ No | ❌ No |

For most people, especially in growing markets, a QR code is the smarter choice — it works on any phone and costs nothing extra to add to your existing card.

## Frequently Asked Questions

No. The built-in camera app on all modern iPhones and Android phones can scan QR codes directly — no third-party app needed.

**What if I change my phone number?**
You'll need to generate a new QR code with the updated details. This is why printing in small batches first is wise if your details change frequently.
