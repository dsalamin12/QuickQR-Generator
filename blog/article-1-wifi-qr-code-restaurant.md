## What Is a WiFi QR Code?

A WiFi QR code is a special type of QR code that stores your network name (SSID) and password. When a customer scans it, their phone automatically connects to your WiFi — just like tapping "Connect" on a saved network.

It works on:
- All iPhones (iOS 11 and above)
- All Android phones (Android 10 and above)
- No special app required — the built-in camera does it

## Tips for the Best Results

**Use high error correction.** If you add your logo to the QR code, the app automatically switches to High error correction — this ensures the code stays scannable even with a logo covering part of it.

**Test before printing.** Always scan your QR code with your own phone before you print 100 copies. Open your camera app, point it at the code, and confirm it connects to your WiFi.

**Keep the background light.** High contrast between dots and background makes scanning faster and more reliable. Black dots on white background always works best.

**Shorter passwords scan faster.** While not always practical, shorter WiFi passwords produce simpler QR codes that scan from further away.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a WiFi QR code safe?**
Yes. The password is stored inside the QR code pattern itself — it's the same password you already share with guests. Anyone who can see the QR code could also ask you for the password anyway. The QR code just makes it convenient.

**Does the QR code expire?**
No. QuickQR creates static QR codes that never expire. Your WiFi QR code will work for as long as your network password stays the same. If you change your password, simply generate a new QR code.

**What if a customer's phone can't scan it?**
Older phones (pre-2018) may not support WiFi QR codes. For those customers, keep the password written down as a backup. But for the vast majority of modern smartphones, it works seamlessly.

**Can I change the colours to match my restaurant branding?**
Absolutely — and we recommend it. A branded QR code that matches your logo colours looks intentional and professional, not like a generic afterthought.
