How to Add a QR Code to Your Email Signature (Free Guide)

QuickQR Guide  ·  Free  ·  5 min read

Your email signature is seen by every person you email. Most signatures have a name, title, and phone number. That's fine. But adding a QR code turns your signature into something interactive — one scan and the recipient can save your contact, open WhatsApp, or visit your website, all on their phone.

Why Add a QR Code to Your Email Signature?

Most people read email on their computer but want to act on their phone. Switching devices is friction. A QR code removes that friction entirely.

  • Client wants to save your number → scan → contact saved in 3 seconds
  • Client wants to WhatsApp you → scan → WhatsApp opens with your number
  • Contact wants to visit your site → scan → website opens on their phone

Step 1: Choose What Your QR Code Will Do

QR TypeBest For
vCard (Contact)Letting people save your full contact info
WhatsAppInviting clients to message you directly
URL (Website)Driving people to your portfolio or LinkedIn
EmailOpening a pre-addressed email to you

Recommendation: Use a vCard QR code — it contains your name, phone, email, company, and job title all in one scan.

Step 2: Create at QuickQR

Go to QuickQR, select the 📇 vCard tab (or WhatsApp/URL as needed), fill in your details, and download as PNG — the right format for email signatures.

Step 3: Design Tips for Email

  • Use black dots on white background — most reliable at small sizes
  • Skip the logo for email — clean code scans more reliably when small
  • Download PNG at full resolution — it will display sharply at 80–100px wide in the signature

Step 4: Add to Gmail

  1. Open Gmail → gear icon → See all settings
  2. Scroll to the Signature section
  3. Click the Insert Image icon → upload your QR code PNG
  4. Resize to roughly 80–100px wide
  5. Click Save Changes

Step 4 (Outlook): Add to Outlook

  1. File → Options → Mail → Signatures
  2. Edit your signature → click the Insert Picture button
  3. Select your QR code PNG → resize to ~80px wide
  4. Save

Step 5: Add a Label

Add one short line above or below the QR code: "Scan to save my contact" or "Scan to WhatsApp me". This one line doubles the number of people who actually use it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the QR code show in plain-text emails?

No — plain-text emails don't support images. The QR code appears in HTML-formatted emails, which is the default for all modern email clients.

Does the recipient need an app to scan it?

No. The built-in camera on all modern iPhones and Android phones scans QR codes directly.
