## Why Add a QR Code to Your Email Signature?

Most people read email on their computer but want to take action on their phone — saving a contact, visiting a website, or sending a WhatsApp message. Switching between devices is friction. A QR code removes that friction entirely.

**Practical scenarios:**

- You email a potential client. They want to save your number. They scan your QR → contact saved in 3 seconds.
- You send a proposal. The client wants to WhatsApp you a quick question. They scan → WhatsApp opens with your number.
- You follow up after a meeting. The contact scans your email signature QR → your website opens on their phone.

It's a small addition that makes a professional impression and makes everything easier for your recipient.

## Step 3: Design It for Email Use

Email signatures are small, so your QR code needs to be clean and simple:

- **Keep it small but scannable** — In your email signature, the QR code image will typically display at around 80–100 pixels wide. This is fine for screen display, but the actual downloaded file should be high resolution for sharp rendering.
- **Use simple colours** — Black dots on white background is the most reliable at small sizes. Avoid complex gradients in email signatures.
- **Skip the logo for small sizes** — A logo inside a QR code needs the code to be large enough to display clearly. For email signatures, a clean code without a logo scans more reliably.

Once happy with the design, click **Download PNG** — PNG is the right format for email signatures.

## Step 4: Add the QR Code to Your Email Signature

### In Gmail:

1. Open Gmail → click the **gear icon** (Settings) → **See all settings**
2. Scroll down to the **Signature** section
3. Click **Create new** (or edit your existing signature)
4. Position your cursor where you want the QR code (usually the bottom right)
5. Click the **Insert image** icon in the signature toolbar
6. Upload your QR code PNG file
7. Resize it to roughly 80–100px wide using the image size options
8. Click **Save Changes** at the bottom

### In Outlook (Classic):

1. Open Outlook → go to **File → Options → Mail → Signatures**
2. Select your signature to edit (or create a new one)
3. Place your cursor where you want the QR code
4. Click the **Insert Picture** button (image icon in the signature editor)
5. Select your downloaded QR code PNG
6. Right-click the image → **Picture** → **Size** tab → set the width to about 80px
7. Click **OK** and save

### In Apple Mail:

1. Go to **Mail → Settings → Signatures**
2. Select your account and signature
3. Drag and drop your QR code PNG image into the signature editing area
4. Resize as needed

- *"Scan to WhatsApp me"*
- *"Scan to visit my website"*

This one line doubles the number of people who actually use it.

## Tips for Professional Results

**Test before sending.** After setting up, send a test email to yourself, open it on your phone, and scan the QR code to confirm it works correctly.

**Check on different email clients.** Some corporate email systems strip images. If you're emailing enterprise clients, confirm the QR code displays for them.

**Use consistent branding.** If your business has specific brand colours, apply them to the QR code dots to match your signature's overall look.

**One QR code is enough.** Don't add multiple QR codes to a single signature — it looks cluttered and confuses the recipient about what to do.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will the QR code show up in plain-text emails?**
No. Plain-text emails don't support images. The QR code only appears in HTML-formatted emails, which is the default for most modern email clients.

**Does the recipient need an app to scan it?**
No. The built-in camera app on all modern iPhones and Android phones scans QR codes directly. No separate app required.

**Will this work in forwarded emails?**
Yes — the QR code image travels with the email when forwarded, as long as the forwarded email is in HTML format.
